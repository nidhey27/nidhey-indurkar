Can Money afford Happiness?

Hey guys! In this blog, I would like to talk about the significant connection between happiness and money. How many of you think that happiness cannot survive without money? Perhaps a few of the readers think money was never an essential factor for the existence of happiness, am I right? Yet, I would say in my opinion, Happiness and Money are two sides of our life without which it’s difficult to imagine our life. As we move toward modernization, money’s worth increases drastically in nature; You might have heard one key phrase in today's world “Everything comes with a price!”.

So, Can happiness also have a price tag?
Can we Buy Happiness with Money?

When you look at the headline, don't these questions cross your mind? So let’s try answering these questions by simply noticing the general happenings in our surroundings in different environments…

The basic question that arises throughout the topic would be “CAN MONEY BUY HAPPINESS?” The answer to this question will differ from person to person. If you ask those with excess money, they would say, "Yes, you can buy happiness with money". On the same note if you ask poverty-stricken people, they might behave as if money was never an issue in life, to begin with. It’s just you need to know how to be satisfied with what’s given. Their statements would be like - “Yeah sab moh Maya hai, Paiso se khushiya nahi kharidi ja sakti… Khushiya pyaar se milti hai, Khushiya pariwar se milti hai… and so on.”

“Whoever said money can’t buy happiness simply didn’t know where to go shopping.”--Gertrude stein

Exactly, people who say that money can’t buy happiness have apparently never used any! What is the point of lying about being satisfied, while struggling to earn money to fulfill the necessities of your family? Can you be happy if your child is hungry, or if you are in debt? Of course not! This proves that money and happiness are interconnected with each other.

Nowadays, the time has radically changed - people’s average income has increased, and even ways to earn and spend money have amplified. As a result, we must learn today's generation's ways of earning and spending money. So that, we can live life to the fullest and use our well-earned money properly to accomplish our daily needs in every aspect of life.

“Money can buy the happiness, because the generation has been changed.” – Unknown

Money is the solution for any materialistic issues -

Money can solve the different problems in your life…

For a commoner, what are the basic issues in everyday life?

If only we could buy a decent sized house in a pleasant area for the family. If only we could afford a car for daily transportation. If only I could get admission to a reputed college. If only I could have a lump sum amount of money to start my own business, and so on… That is, if we observe these situations closely, the common problems of life are money-related. For instance, I want to buy a house; if I have the finances, I can buy a luxurious villa or bungalow at any location I prefer, such as the seafront. Regardless of how expensive a specialist's services are, I could have afforded to receive proper treatment for any illness. If you have funds, you can establish your own business or you can study at any eminent institute. Overall, many of the problems in our life can be solved with money.

✔ Purpose of Wealth: It is common to experience momentary happiness when we think of having money, like being on vacation, having an iPhone, paying for a BMW, etc. Moreover, if you explore the deepest possibilities of money, you will realize that money can give you all the ‘THINGS’ that can make you happy. The thing is money can help you achieve Freedom, Security, Healthy relationships, Better education, and a Bright Future. If we summarize, the basic purpose of Wealth is to improve our Living Standards.

Okay, to get a better grasp of my ideas, let us practice a simple simulation: Just imagine that you are having a luxurious life, you are wearing expensive clothes, your child is studying at renowned universities, and you can daily have healthy meals four times a day. How do you feel now? Wow feels good, right? Okay, now assume that you came into reality. You belong to a poor family, where a roof and clothes are out of the question, since you cannot even afford two times the bread. Woof, quite scary! Now, you ask yourself- “Which life would you prefer?”

“Money can’t buy happiness but it can buy you the misery you prefer.” –Groucho Marx

Money makes life easier; In an easy life, it becomes easier to achieve a state of happiness. When you don't have to worry about your basic needs, then you can explore many different aspects of life like learning, reading, spirituality, and many more things which establishes peace in your subconscious mind by reducing the stress from your daily life. Maybe you wouldn’t have noticed it, but money can also give us extra time. Having extra time means we have time to improve ourselves by working on ourselves or to enjoy our hobbies.

✔ Money aids to build a healthy mental system: Even though you have paid the EMI for 5 years, you still need to pay more for the next 5 years to complete the full payment of your 3 BHK flat. Along with that, you have to pay your children's academic fees and their general expenses from your salary each month. Additionally, being in a joint family (which is common in Asian culture) you need to take care of your parents’ meditations and other medical or health-related equipment. Is it possible to have all this going on in your life and not feel stressed? What is the probability of that person enjoying his life? Are you sure that he/she is ok with not getting to spend some free time for himself/herself or his/her family and friends?

Instead, having enough money in our pocket decreases most of the stress-threatening factors in our life. If money can solve most of the problems and can free us from a stress full life, we can assume that money does help us to be happy. Some people are struggling to earn enough to cover their daily expenses, while others are still stressing out about how they can earn a bigger amount of money or how they can expand their businesses to boost their income source, although they already have enough money. This is called being greedy. One should always feel grateful if one can afford a comfortable life. If it is challenging, think about those who can’t even afford a fan for the summer season, or how tough it could be to sleep on a mattress without having a blanket to resist the cold breezes in winter, or maybe the floating house in rainy days could do.

You can buy comfort with money. Take an example of cars like BMW, Audi, and Rolls Royce, they charge you for comfort. Being comfortable could be less stressful.

Money gives you the steering of your life in your hands. If you want to travel to another city urgently and if you have enough money, you can book a plane. Even if the plane isn't available for whatever reason, you can book a cab and you can arrive at the destination. Money opens multiple doors for you.

“If money can’t buy happiness, then I guess I’ll have to rent it.”— Unknown

Happiness is a state of mind, is it?

Money-related tension can fill a calm person with stress and despair. That's why money is necessary for a stress-free and calm mind. In an interview, Indian actor Shah Rukh Khan once said- “Don’t become a philosopher before you become rich!”.

As said, happiness is a state of mind. I agree - being happy is entirely up to you. But this state comes only after stability; stability of being employed or having a business, stability of having a proper house to stay in, stability of your children getting a proper education, and stability of fulfilling daily necessities. For all this, one should be “CAPABLE ENOUGH WITH MONEY” to bring themselves to a state of stability.

✔ Diversity of Pleasure and Happiness: So when you get an appraisal at work, or if your child has spoken his/her first word, or your dearest younger sister got her first salary, or you get the news that one of your closest friends is getting married, or maybe your elder brother is going to become a dad in a few months, how would you feel? Sounds thrilling, right? This short-lived happiness is known as Momentary happiness. Momentary happiness is an emotion of pleasure. Then, what is long-term happiness? The condition of sustained happiness means the state of happiness. Like having healthy relationships, pursuing your dream career, accomplishing the biggest goal in your life, or getting to learn about the reasons for your existence, etc. It is very important to analyze the difference between pleasure and happiness so that we can understand what the limitations of pecuniary (monetary) happiness are.

Many people get confused between pleasure and happiness and think that both are the same. However, pleasure is external and happiness is internal. Pleasure can be achieved with a substance while happiness cannot be achieved with any substance. Happiness is different from pleasure in some ways, even though it seems similar. Money can be of much help to those who are looking for pleasure but there are some drawbacks to pleasure. Pleasure is time-limited. Pleasure often does not last long, since attention quickly moves to other matters. The feeling of being happy fades, when the individual is unable to access the resource or the interest in the resource ends or their attention is diverted. Sometimes, too much of something stops being enjoyable. That's why people who use money for pleasure, at a certain point, think that money can't bring happiness. The extremes of pleasure lead to addiction, whether to a substance or behavior.

Money can bring you both happiness and pleasure at the same time, how? If you have money, society will compliment you, respect you and give attention to you whereas when you don’t have any money, your social value becomes NIL. They say people always salute the rising sun, and not the setting one, but how long will this pretentious respect and appreciation keep you happy??

If you spend on experiences, then experiences become part of your memories. Your memories of those experiences make you happy whenever you recall them. So if you want your happiness to last for a longer duration or period then fill your memory bank with such experiences, which will keep you happy. We do a huge mistake by buying/spending on those materialistic things like iPhones, expensive clothes, etc which give us momentary pleasure whereas we don't realize the importance of experience. Experiences make your life meaningful, while materialistic things give you momentary happiness.

Money helps you to make others happy.

Money also helps you to make others smile. If your child wants to buy toys or you want to buy a gift for your partner or parents, or even if you want to treat a beggar, everything requires money. Therefore, in order to keep your loved ones happy, to help the needy, or to do any sort of social work, you need money. When the people involved are satisfied, you also get content. It is scientifically proven that by making others happy, you get some frequency waves generated in your brain due to which you get satisfaction and achieve inner happiness. If you can’t believe it, try to help a needy poor person, or anyone. Nevertheless, you can only aid them when you have enough money. If your needs are higher than your income then you won't be able to help others, and if anyhow you manage to help others then you need to lower your desires which will lead to a decrease in self-happiness.

In this case, both prerequisites are relevant: To give someone something, we need money (which is a materialistic act) and to give them a good life, we feel happy (therefore, it is a state of mind)
“They say money can’t buy happiness, darling! But it does allow you to give it away!” –Freddie Mercury
Wrapping up

In conclusion, most of life's materialistic problems can be solved by money.. Still, it is ultimately up to us to do what we can to make ourselves happy. Let's all do our very best to satisfy our desires and bring that stability to our lives. You do not need money to live a happy life, but instead of waiting for the "RIGHT" opportunity, you should always be open to learning and trying various things to adapt to the current technological era. Instead of blaming money or forming the theory that money can't buy happiness, think progressively, take actions, and develop a magnetic personality. This will attract both money and happiness to you. Rather than chasing happiness and money, you must develop your worth so that you can incorporate everything into leading a happy life.
