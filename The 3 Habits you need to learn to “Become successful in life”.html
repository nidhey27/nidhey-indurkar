The 3 Habits: “Foundation of Success”

  1. Learn to sell: The phrase anticipates how to sell yourself. Oh! What I just said would sound disgusting, but it’s a fact. To be more precise, you need to sell (demonstrate) your skills to the interviewer if you want to earn the job. You have to sell your products to as many clients as you can to raise your profit. That means, to gain something, you need to sell something which belongs to you; it can be your skills, your work, your products, etc.

  2. Choose Wisely: You have to choose wisely what is good and what is bad for you. You should know how to figure out which steps would take you closer to your goals. In addition, look around your friend circle where you frequently spend time. Spend your time with level-up people. Spending your day with similar level people can never help you to improve. In fact, even if you try to grow in such surroundings, there is a probability that you will get dragged down by those people. In such cases, you will get to hear a phrases like “chodna yaar ye sab kya kar raha hai chill kartein hai”. Being closed to well-experienced people encourages you to learn more and explore your knowledge in a proper place. In short, I would like to shed some light on this famous quote, “If you are the smartest guy in the room, then you are in the wrong room”. This means you won’t be learning anything new when you are the only smartest in the room.

  3. Sow the seeds of multiple income streams: To become financially independent and wealthy simultaneously, you need to work for various income streams. You can’t be affluent by a single source of income. For example, you can start a side hustle, a small business, or you can do stock trading; invest in real estate apart from your job.

The habit that I practice a lot is “Positive Affirmations”. What is a positive affirmation? Basically, initiating or finishing your day by saying something good to yourself is positive affirmations. You would have seen lots of people talking with their image in a mirror and telling themselves that you’re awesome, you are great! And other pleasant phrases to themselves. This concept works and trains your mind to think that you are good enough or capable to achieve whatever you aspire. Now the most important thing for everybody is confidence. The moment you lose confidence, all three habits will be affected negatively. So positive affirmation is the best way to grow your confidence.

Secondly, you need to understand that Life is Hard. Okay, sometimes life can be very hard, hundered’s of bouncers will come your way. Then we can do two things – Either we get hit by a bouncer, sit back and cry, blame ourselves, other people or we can come back like Virat Kohli, put the helmet on, come back and hit a century. So either we can cry, or we can find the solution. When you get this kind of attitude, you get more confident. Who's gonna stop you from becoming successful? Nobody, but you.

And lastly, I would like to say imagine yourself in the next 5 years, Where do you see yourself in the next 5 Years? What are your goals for the next 5 years? Just keep working for those goals and give your best. Think that I want to be in that position in the next five years. Even if you can't reach the goal don’t be disappointd because at least you have tried your best; so love the procedure and keep trying with determination. Just remember that whenever you face failure you get to learn new things from your mistakes. Learn from your mistakes. Things can go wrong, screw it and instead of crying try to find solution.

And I would like to conclude by “To baat aaysi hai ki, Har Jeet to Parinam hai, Par koshish krna to, humara kaam hai”
